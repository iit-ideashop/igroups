Introduction

The IPRO® Program prepares students for the practical challenges they will face in a changing workplace by emulating a cross-functional team environment.  The program engages multidisciplinary teams of students in semester-long projects based on real-world topics from sponsors that reflect the diversity of the workplace: corporations, entrepreneurial ventures, non-profit organizations, government agencies, and university researchers. Teams may include students from all academic levels (sophomore through graduate school), and across IIT’s professional programs (engineering, science, business, law, psychology, design, and architecture). Integration of both vertical (bridging academic levels) and horizontal (bridging professional programs) dimensions within a project team experience is distinctive in higher education today.  Through this program, students have the opportunity to develop a unique portfolio of real-world experiences that will help focus their academic efforts to career directions that best fit their aptitude and interest. The grading, deliverables and assessment process are designed to emulate the type of project responsibilities, cross-functional collaboration experience and team process evaluation that professionals encounter during their careers.

 

IPRO 339-- Specific Project Information

Title:                      Benchmarking IPRO Processes and Exploring Cutting Edge Technologies for IPRO Team Projects

Sponsor:                IPRO PRogram

Description:         The IPRO 339 team will have two main tasks: (1) research and investigate such leading edge and cross-cutting technologies as nanotechnology, wireless mobile software/hardware, sensors and instrumentation, process simulation, visualization and gaming software, autonomous robots, human-computer interaction design, etc. for possible application by various IPRO teams, and (2)implement a benchmarking system for the IPRO program itself [designed by the Fall 2003 IPRO 339 team], with the goal of discovering best practices in team-based projects at other universities that can be adapted for the IPRO Program.

One group task is to investigate the latest cutting edge research and technology areas and write a position paper and recommendations on the potential for such research and technologies in the context of all of IIT's IPRO project teams. Based on their analysis and recommendations, and working with faculty and students on other IPRO teams, students on this team can be a resource for all IPRO teams as a "window" on developments in emerging fields that can enhance the knowledge base available to all IPRO teams. To support their investigations, students will also actively identify and work with companies who are developing or commercializing these research and technology areas and involve such companies in IPRO activities [sponsorship, hiring interns, the IPRO Projects Day Conference, etc.].

The second group task involves comparing (i.e., benchmarking) the IPRO project course at IIT with project team courses at other prominent engineering universities. Through benchmarking, an organization systematically evaluates itself and its competition in order to uncover the process strategies that can lead to the highest levels of performance. Many organizations have adopted benchmarking as a means of continuous performance improvement including companies such as Proctor & Gamble, Motorola, Toyota, Texas Instruments, the U.S. Department of Defense and Xerox.

The IPRO Program is establishing a formal continuous performance improvement process to understand what process improvements or changes will better enable the IPRO program to achieve its institutional and learning objectives while continually improving the quality of the program for students, faculty, and sponsors. This is also an important tool for IIT in meeting its IPRO Program assessment responsibilities for the ABET (Accreditation Board for Engineering and Technology) EC2000 Criteria. Students enrolled in IPRO 339 will learn not only about industry critical consulting and performance measurement processes, but will help apply their learning to enhance the IPRO project experience for all IIT students and further establish IIT as a leader in interprofessional education.

 

Interprofessional Course Learning Objectives

The interprofessional course develops the following cross-competencies, skills and perspectives:

  • Development and strengthening of Multidisciplinary Team Building and Teamwork practices
  • Effective use of Project Management methodology
  • Effective use of Verbal, Written, and Visual Communication methods
  • Effective engagement in Real World Problem Solving that integrates complex issues
  • Awareness of the Benefits and Desire for Life Long Learning
  • Awareness and Application of Business Planning Principles (Entrepreneurial IPRO project teams)

Grading and Assessment

There are two components of a student's grade -- the team's performance and the individual's performance as part of the team. All of the assignments listed and described in the following “Assignment” section will contribute to the determination of a grade for the team's overall performance. In addition, the contribution of the individual student to the team's work can determine (a) whether s/he should be given recognition through a higher grade for exceptional work, (b) whether s/he performed at the norm of the team, or (c) whether s/he should be penalized for failing to meet their obligations. Ideally, and at the discretion of the instructor, all members of the team will provide input to the individual component of a student's grade by completing a peer evaluation form at the mid-point of the semester and at the end of the semester during a team meeting. A Peer Evaluation Form provided by the IPRO Program Office provides one method for an instructor and team to conduct a peer evaluation. This form, or another format that the instructor may wish to use, gives all members of the team the opportunity to provide feedback to the faculty on their perceptions of the contributions of their teammates to the overall team effort. Peer evaluation has been found to be a helpful tool at the mid-point of the semester to provide individual students with feedback so that they have an opportunity to improve their performance during the second half of the semester. The results of this feedback can be an important input in assigning individual grades.  Other assessment tools for the interprofessional project course include (1) a student and faculty survey that reflects on IPRO learning objectives as part of the end-of-semester team debriefing, (2) the IIT course evaluation form, (3) surveys of sponsors, alumni and employers, and (4) IPRO Day judging.

II. INDIVIDUAL PERFORMANCE SCORE (IPS) (50 points A student’s Individual Performance Score (IPS) is developed from two sources: the student’s peers and the lead faculty with a maximum of 20 points for each source.  The IPS has three components, based on completion of the IPRO Team Member Performance Review form by the team members and the faculty, with relative weighting as shown below:

A.      Average Peer Score (PS) (20 points maximum)

Team members evaluate each other’s performance in an objective and confidential fashion using the IPRO Team Member Performance Review form (see attached) which covers two categories: Individual Responsibility to the Team and Individual Effort & Achievement. For any given student, the PS is the average of the individual scores assigned by that student’s peers on the team, excluding that student.

B.      Faculty Score (FS) (30 points maximum)

The lead faculty and team leader also evaluate team member performance using the IPRO Team Member Performance Appraisal form (see attached). As faculty complete the evaluation, they include consideration of each team member’s individual self-appraisal (IS) and each team member’s narrative that supports their self-appraisal.

 

 

IPRO Assignments

Completed IPRO assignments (e.g., deliverables associated with the IPRO Team Portfolio) are reviewed by the instructor and then submitted electronically using the IPRO Project Reporting System (PRS) web site (http://webservices.iit.edu/ipro/pms/smsv). The IPRO Program Office reviews the reports submitted by all of the IPRO teams and maintains an archive of materials for assessment and future reference. The IPRO Project Reporting System web site offers guidelines, methodologies and examples that are helpful in planning the various assignments. Team Portfolios from previous semesters are available for review in the IPRO Program Office.

 

The assignments that are integrated in the IPRO Team Portfolio are defined below:

 

1.       The IPRO Course Syllabus (including the Schedule of Events) is provided by the faculty leading each team. It may be customized by individual faculty to suit his/her purpose with respect to learning objectives, grading and other issues, as well as specific issues related to a sponsoring organization, including goals, tasks, confidentiality and intellectual property. The syllabus is made available by the instructor at the IPRO PRS web site.

 

2.       The Project Plan prompts the teams at the beginning of the semester to quickly and properly formulate a statement of their project objectives, background on the project, methodology, expected results, schedule of tasks and milestone events, and assigned responsibilities for each individual on the team. While the plan may change as additional work is done, an initial effort at creating a project plan is a valuable way to begin to seriously and methodically focus on the issues that have to be addressed to accomplish the desired goals. The Project Plan is a useful tool to facilitate organizing the team into groups, assigning tasks and responsibilities to individuals, and establishing a process for accountability that can guide the team throughout the semester.  Guidelines for the Project Plan are found at the IPRO PRS web site.

 

3.       The Mid-Term Progress Report & Oral Review formalizes the process of midterm reporting by the team to the faculty and the sponsor, thereby providing an opportunity for feedback and dialogue among all concerned before the term ends. The IPRO Mid-Term Progress Report prompts the team to review its approach and progress toward goals, and assure that sponsor input and feedback is maintained. Material from both the Project Plan and Mid-Term Progress Report become important starting points for the Final Report, saving time and effort at the conclusion of the project. Guidelines for the Mid-Term Progress Report are found at the IPRO PRS system web site. IPRO teams are also encouraged to give a Mid-Term Project Oral Review as a way to gain experience in organizing their team presentation. This also provides an opportunity to obtain feedback and ideas covering your project from a panel of faculty and staff.  At the end of February, the IPRO Program Office will ask IPRO teams if they would like to give a mid-term presentation during the weeks of March 8 or March 22. 

 

4.       The Web Site is an active resource for the team to manage its business during the semester, create a record of the team's work during the semester, and provide a record of the team's accomplishments at the end of the semester. Depending on the nature of the project, the web site may itself offer a significant technical challenge to create and maintain. Some web sites may also be maintained following the conclusion of the semester, and become an important resource for students interested in joining a follow-on project team or for the IPRO Program Office in showcasing completed IPRO projects for prospective sponsors. Guidelines for the web site are included in Section 4 of the IPRO Team Portfolio. (Teams are also required to establish and use a Yahoo Groups account for their own benefit as well as for use by the IPRO Program Office in communicating effectively with all students enrolled in IPRO team course sections.)

 

5.       The Final Oral Presentation is delivered at the IPRO Projects Day Conference.  Representatives from industry, trustees, alumni, faculty, staff and student peers judge the oral presentations. A separate panel of judges representing the entrepreneurial and venture financing community judges the EnPRO oral presentations. IPRO teams may also give their oral presentation to sponsors and at other venues and special events that the university may have. Guidelines for the presentation are found at the IPRO PRS web site. Updated instructions and a schedule of presentations are distributed to teams in preparation for the IPRO Projects Day Conference later in the term.

 

6.       A Professional-Style Poster is displayed at the IPRO Projects Day Conference in an all-day poster session with all IPRO teams in the Hermann Union Building. Guidelines for the poster are found at the IPRO PRS system web site.  Updated instructions are distributed to teams in preparation for IPRO Projects Day later in the term. 

 

7.       A One-Page Abstract is provided for the IPRO Projects Day Conference as a handout for people attending the oral presentation or visiting the poster session. The one-page abstract is also used for various promotional purposes by the IPRO Program Office. Guidelines for the one-page abstract are found at the IPRO PRS web site.

 

8.       A Final Project Report is delivered by the end of the semester. The final report summarizes both the work of the team and its accomplishments as well as the process of working as a team. Guidelines for the one-page abstract are found at the IPRO PRS web site. In the case of Entrepreneurial IPRO (EnPRO) teams, the final report is represented by the Business Plan, which follows a prescribed format.

 

9.       Team Minutes and Interprofessional Perspectives is a "living document" that compiles a complete record of the team's activities and documentation throughout the semester. During the semester, this section is used to keep an up-to-date list of team members and their contact information, compile the agenda and minutes of team meetings and sponsor meetings, retain information gathered through fieldtrips and information searches, keep track of professionals and organizations contacted for various purposes, document laboratory procedures and data sheets, keep a record of pictures of team meetings, field trips, prototypes, etc. If some of these materials are organized in separate folders and file drawers, this section should contain an index to these materials and where they are located. This section also contains the results of the team’s critical evaluation of the various interprofessional issues that are relevant to the project.

 

10.    An IPRO Debriefing Session is conducted with each IPRO team at the end of the semester following the IPRO Day Conference to reflect on the learning achievements of their IPRO project, including the nature of the challenges and successes that the team realized. The debriefing is an hour long session that includes completing a learning objectives survey and participating in a discussion in which students and the instructor reflect on their IPRO project experiences. This has tremendous value for the IPRO Program Office and faculty in assessing and continually improving the interprofessional project course experience.
